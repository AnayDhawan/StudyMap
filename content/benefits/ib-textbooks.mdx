# Recommended textbooks per subject

Choosing the right textbook makes a huge difference. Here is what experienced students recommend for each major subject.

## How to use this guide

- **Textbook**: main reference for theory
- **Revision guide**: condensed summary and practice
- **Past papers**: exam-style practice; critical for final prep
- **Tips**: subject-specific notes on how to study effectively

Most textbooks are available second-hand on Amazon, Flipkart, or local bookshops at 30–50% discount.

## IB Subjects

### Mathematics (HL and SL)

**Main textbooks**
- Oxford IB Mathematics Analysis and Approaches (Fannon, Tanenbaum)
- Pearson Mathematics: Applications and Interpretation

**Revision guides**
- IB Study Guide for Mathematics: Analysis and Approaches (Fannon, Tanenbaum)

**Past papers and practice**
- IB Past Papers (via MyIB portal or IB websites)
- Mathematics Standard Level and Higher Level exam papers (last 10 years)
- Khan Academy for concept gaps

**Tips**
- Work past papers under timed conditions from September onward
- Distinguish between calculator and non-calculator sections
- Master proof techniques early (induction, contradiction, by cases)

### Physics (HL and SL)

**Main textbooks**
- Tsokos Physics for the IB Diploma (standard reference)
- Oxford IB Physics Course Companion (Allum, Klaus)

**Revision guides**
- Physics Study Guide for the IB Diploma (Tsokos)

**Past papers and practice**
- IB Physics past papers (data booklet essential)
- Khan Academy physics playlists
- Physics Tutor Online (YouTube channel)

**Tips**
- The data booklet is allowed in exams; memorize only key equations
- Practice free-response questions early; they account for 40% of marks
- Labs and uncertainty analysis matter — IA prep starts in Year 1

### Chemistry (HL and SL)

**Main textbooks**
- Catrin Brown Chemistry for the IB Diploma (comprehensive and clear)
- Pearson Chemistry: IB Course Companion

**Revision guides**
- Chemistry Study Guide for the IB Diploma (Brown)

**Past papers and practice**
- IB Chemistry past papers
- ChemistryAid.com (free notes and video walkthroughs)
- Khan Academy chemistry series

**Tips**
- Organic chemistry accounts for 20% of marks; start early
- Memorize the periodic table trends early
- Past papers are essential from October onward

### Biology (HL and SL)

**Main textbooks**
- Allott and Mindorff Biology for the IB Diploma (clear and thorough)
- Pearson Biology IB Course Companion

**Revision guides**
- Biology Study Guide for the IB Diploma (Allott, Mindorff)

**Past papers and practice**
- IB Biology past papers
- Khan Academy AP Biology (covers most IB content)
- Biology Online Tutor (YouTube)

**Tips**
- The syllabus is vast; prioritize core option topics early
- Genetics and ecology appear in almost every paper
- IA skills (data analysis, uncertainty) matter from September

### English (HL and SL)

**Main textbooks**
- English B for the IB Diploma: Course Companion (if doing Language B)
- English A: Literature and Composition (if doing Language A)

**Revision guides**
- IB Exam Preparation Guides for English (subject-specific)

**Practice materials**
- Prescribed and wider reading lists from your school
- Past papers for essay and commentary sections
- The Economist, BBC Learning English (for English B)

**Tips**
- Read prescribed books early (August of Year 1)
- Wider reading = 20% of marks; read strategically
- Essay technique matters more than knowledge

### History (HL and SL)

**Main textbooks**
- Oxford IB History Course Companion (clear, factual)
- Pearson History IB Diploma (comprehensive)

**Revision guides**
- Subject-specific revision guides matching your prescribed topics

**Past papers and practice**
- IB History past papers (exam-specific essay formats)
- Documentaries and primary source collections

**Tips**
- Know your prescribed topics inside-out; exams are specific
- Historiography (different interpretations) matters at HL
- Essays require evidence and structured analysis

### Geography (HL and SL)

**Main textbooks**
- Prosser and Dunphy Geography for the IB Diploma (comprehensive)
- Oxford IB Geography Course Companion

**Revision guides**
- Geography Study Guide for the IB Diploma

**Practice materials**
- Case study research (cities, regions, systems)
- Past papers for structured essay answers
- Google Earth for spatial context

**Tips**
- Case studies are critical; develop 3–5 detailed ones per topic
- Synoptic thinking (linking concepts) is tested heavily
- Data interpretation (maps, graphs, images) is half the exam

- Cambridge IGCSE Combined and Coordinated Sciences (Jane Bailey and others)
- Hodder Education Science revision guides

### History and Geography

- Cambridge IGCSE History and Geography workbooks (matched to syllabus)

## General strategies

1. **Textbook + past papers, not textbook alone.** Textbooks teach concepts; past papers teach exam technique.
2. **Use second-hand copies.** Textbooks cost ₹1,500–3,000 new; buy used for ₹700–1,500.
3. **Share with classmates.** Form a rotation system or photocopy key chapters.
4. **Exploit YouTube.** Channels like Khan Academy, Crash Course, and subject specialists often explain concepts better than textbooks.
5. **Buy revision guides only after Halloween.** Early in Year 1, use textbooks. Switch to revision guides 6 months before exams.

## Where to find books

- **Physical**: local school book stores, Amazon.in, Flipkart
- **Second-hand**: OLX, Facebook Marketplace, used book shops in your area
- **Digital PDFs**: check your school library portal; some titles are available free
- **Rentals**: sometimes cheaper than buying for one-time exams

The best textbook is one you will actually read. Choose one you like the style of, even if it is not the "official" one.

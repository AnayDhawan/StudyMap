# Subject selection guide

Choosing your subjects shapes your diploma, your transcript, and sometimes your eligibility for university programs. Here is how to think about it strategically.

## IB subject selection strategy

You choose 6 subjects (normally 3 HL, 3 SL), distributed across subject groups:

| Group | Subjects | Choose |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 1. English/Literature | English A HL/SL | 1 |
| 2. Second Language | French, Spanish, Mandarin, etc. | 1 |
| 3. Social Sciences | History, Geography, Economics, Psychology, Business | 1 |
| 4. Sciences | Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Environmental Science | 1 |
| 5. Mathematics | Math: Analysis & Approaches or Applications & Interpretation (HL/SL) | 1 |
| 6. The Sixth | Repeat any group above, or take an elective (Art, Music, CS, Sports) | 1 |

### The HL vs SL decision

**HL (Higher Level): 240 hours**
- Deeper theory, more proof, harder exams
- Counts more toward selective university admissions
- More IAs (Internal Assessments)

**SL (Standard Level): 150 hours**
- Covers core concepts and application
- Sufficient for most university programs
- Lighter workload in exam season

**Strategy**: Choose HL in subjects you either (a) love, (b) need for university, or (c) are your strongest in. Don't take 3 hard HL subjects unless you have a specific reason.

### Science subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, or combined?

**Physics HL** — For engineering, astrophysics, physics, or demanding CS programs.
- Most abstract, hardest for struggling students
- Heavy maths requirement
- Best if you like problem-solving and theory

**Chemistry HL** — For medicine, pharmacy, materials science, or general science programs.
- Middle ground in difficulty
- Organic chemistry can be hard; inorganic and physical are straightforward
- Good balance of theory and practical application

**Biology HL** — For medicine, biology, neuroscience, or applied life sciences.
- More memorization than Physics or Chemistry
- IA focus on experimental design and data analysis
- Good if you like systems thinking and real-world application

**What universities prefer**:
- Medicine: Chemistry HL + Biology HL (Physics SL acceptable)
- Engineering: Physics HL + Math HL (Chemistry negotiable)
- General Science: Any science HL

### Math: Analysis & Approaches vs Applications & Interpretation

**Analysis & Approaches** (more pure math, fewer applications)
- Preferred by: Physics HL, Engineering, Theoretical CS
- Harder if you don't enjoy proof and abstraction
- Better preparation for higher math at university

**Applications & Interpretation** (more data, statistics, modeling)
- Preferred by: Social sciences, Economics, Business, some Engineering
- More accessible if you struggle with pure maths
- Better for practical problem-solving

Both are calculus-based. Your university major will usually specify which one they want; check before choosing.

### Language selection

**Keep going in a language you have studied before** (usually from IGCSE or earlier).
- HL is doable for strong linguists; SL is more common
- Continuity matters more than switching languages

**Consider ease vs usefulness**:
- English A (Literature or Language): easiest; taught in school
- Spanish or French (B): widely spoken; good for travel
- Mandarin or Arabic: harder but rare, stands out on applications
- Classical languages (Latin, Ancient Greek): uncommon but respected

**Rule of thumb**: Take the language you enjoy, not the one you think looks impressive on paper.

### The sixth subject: Safe pick or stretch?

**Safe picks** (easier to combine with hard HL subjects):
- Repeat a language or social science
- Take Art, Music, or Sports Science
- These leave energy for Physics/Math/Chemistry HL

**Stretch picks** (require significant effort):
- Computer Science HL (if you want CS at university)
- Economics HL (if you want business/finance)
- These combine well with Math HL, not as well with 2+ sciences HL

**Personal fit matters more than prestige.** Universities care about your 3 HL subjects; your sixth barely factors into admissions.

## Workload balancing: a practical framework

### Avoid dangerous combinations

❌ Physics HL + Chemistry HL + Math HL + English A HL
- Too much abstract theory + proof work
- Very high burnout risk

❌ All 3 sciences HL
- 80% of your study time to science
- Limited energy for essays, languages, and other subjects

✓ Physics HL + Chemistry SL + Math HL + English A SL + Language SL + Geography SL
- Two hard subjects (Physics, Math)
- Four manageable SL subjects
- Diverse skill mix (theory + application + language + writing)

✓ Chemistry HL + Biology HL + Geography HL + English A SL + Math SL + Language SL
- Hard sciences + geography
- Lighter math and language
- Good for medicine pathways without burning out

### Decision checklist

Before you finalize, ask yourself:

1. **University fit**: Does my top choice program require specific HL subjects? (Check their prerequisites.)
2. **Strength**: Am I in the top 20% of my class in Physics/Chemistry/Biology? If not, consider SL.
3. **Interest**: Will I want to keep studying this subject at university? (If yes, HL. If no, SL.)
4. **Time budget**: Can I manage 4 HL subjects (or 3 very hard ones) and stay sane?
5. **Sixth subject**: Does it open doors (CS for CS programs) or just round out the profile?

## IGCSE subject selection (Years 10–11)

IGCSE choices are less binding than IB, but they signal your direction. You typically choose:

- English Language (mandatory)
- Mathematics (mandatory)
- Sciences (Choose Double Science or 3 separate sciences)
- Humanities (History, Geography, or both)
- Electives (Languages, Business, IT, Art, Music, etc.)

### Combination rules

- **Default**: English, Maths, Double Science, 1 Humanities, 1 Elective = 5 subjects
- **Ambitious**: Add extra Humanities or Languages = 6–7 subjects (not recommended unless very strong in subjects already)
- **Focused**: Fewer subjects but prioritize your strength areas

### Science choice at IGCSE

**Double Science** (2 GCSEs)
- Covers Physics, Chemistry, Biology at the same level
- Faster pace, slightly lower depth
- Standard choice for most schools
- Still eligible for Science HL at IB if you do well

**Separate Sciences** (3 GCSEs: Physics, Chemistry, Biology)
- Deeper content in each
- More time-intensive
- Better preparation for Science HL at IB
- Recommended if you are considering Medicine, Engineering, or Physics

### Setting up for IB success

If you are in IGCSE Year 10–11 and thinking ahead to IB:

- **Do well in Sciences** if you might want Science HL (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)
- **Take Math if available** — never regret being stronger at Maths
- **Include a language** — makes IB Language A easier
- **Keep options open**: don't specialize too early unless certain of your direction

## Final thoughts

1. **Talk to current students and teachers.** They know the actual difficulty and workload better than any guide.
2. **Look at past papers before finalizing.** The exam style will tell you if the subject suits you.
3. **Remember you can change.** Some schools allow subject changes in the first 4–6 weeks of Year 12; check yours.
4. **Balance ambition with sanity.** Getting 6s in 4 HL subjects is better than getting 3s in 6 HL subjects.

Subject selection is about fit, not prestige. Choose subjects where you can excel, not subjects that look impressive on paper.

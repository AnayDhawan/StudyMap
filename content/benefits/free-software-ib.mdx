# Free software for IB and IGCSE students

Most of the software you need for IB and IGCSE is either free or deeply discounted as a student. Here is what to claim and where.

## Development and CS tools

**JetBrains Suite** (free with student email)

- IntelliJ IDEA for Java, Kotlin, and general development
- PyCharm for Python
- WebStorm for web development
- CLion for C++
- All Pro editions, fully free while you are a student

Apply at [jetbrains.com/student](https://www.jetbrains.com/student) with your school email.

**Visual Studio Code** (free)

- Industry standard, no licensing needed
- Download from [code.visualstudio.com](https://code.visualstudio.com)
- Huge ecosystem of free extensions

**GitHub** (free + Pro)

- Free public and private repositories
- GitHub Pro is free with the Student Developer Pack
- GitHub Copilot is free for students via Pro

## Productivity and note-taking

**Notion** (free with student email or upgrade discount)

- Unlimited pages, databases, and workspace members on free plan
- Apply for student upgrade at [notion.so/students](https://www.notion.so/students)
- Free Plus plan features (synced databases, gallery view)

**Microsoft Office 365** (often free via school)

- Ask your school admin; many provide free Office 365 for students
- If not, OneNote and OneDrive are free standalone

**Obsidian** (free for personal use)

- Free cross-platform markdown note-taking
- Syncing requires subscription, but local vault is always free
- [obsidian.md](https://obsidian.md)

## Design tools

**Figma** (free Education plan)

- Industry standard for UI/UX design
- Free Education plan at [figma.com/education](https://figma.com/education)
- Unlimited files, prototyping, and team projects for students

**Canva Pro** (often free via school or heavily discounted)

- Easy graphic design, presentations, social media
- Check if your school provides free access
- Student discount usually available ([canva.com/students](https://canva.com/students))

**Adobe Creative Cloud** (steep discount for students)

- Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro, and 20+ apps
- Student plan is roughly ₹5,000/month (vs. ₹20,000 for adults)
- First month often free to try
- Claim via [adobe.com/creativecloud/buy/student](https://adobe.com/creativecloud/buy/student)

**Krita** (free open source)

- Digital painting and illustration
- No student plan needed — always free
- [krita.org](https://krita.org)

## Math and science tools

**Desmos** (free)

- Graphing calculator, scientific calculator
- Web-based, no login required
- [desmos.com](https://desmos.com)

**GeoGebra** (free)

- Geometry, algebra, calculus visualization
- Desktop app and web version
- [geogebra.org](https://www.geogebra.org)

**Wolfram Alpha** (free tier + student discount)

- Computational knowledge engine
- Free tier is powerful for most student work
- Pro subscription at [wolframalpha.com](https://www.wolframalpha.com)

## Workflow habits

1. Claim everything from the GitHub Student Pack first (it is the foundation)
2. Use your school email wherever possible for instant verification
3. Set calendar reminders to renew before exams — don't let licenses lapse mid-term
4. Mix free tools (VS Code, Desmos, Obsidian) with student discounts (Adobe, Notion) rather than paying full price
5. Check your school's software portal — many licenses are provided free by your institution

The rule: if you have a student email and a vendor has a student program, apply. It usually takes 2 minutes and saves thousands.

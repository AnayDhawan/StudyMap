# Getting the GitHub Student Developer Pack

The GitHub Student Developer Pack is the single best starting point for free tools as a student. One application unlocks dozens of offers from industry partners.

## What you get

- Free GitHub Pro (normally ₹400/month equivalent)
- Free or heavily discounted tiers from 50+ companies
- Cloud credits (DigitalOcean, AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)
- Free development tools (JetBrains, Namecheap, Heroku)
- Design and productivity software discounts

## How to apply

1. Go to [education.github.com/pack](https://education.github.com/pack)
2. Click "Sign up for Student Developer Pack"
3. Sign in to your GitHub account (or create one free)
4. Verify your school affiliation by uploading a photo of your student ID or accepting a verification link sent to your school email
5. GitHub usually approves applications within minutes to a few hours

## Tips

- Use your school email address — it is the fastest path to verification
- If your school uses Microsoft or Google for student accounts, you may already be linked
- Once approved, benefits are valid for 2 years; you can renew on graduation
- Some offers (like AWS credits) expire after 12 months, so claim them early
- Check back every few months — new partner offers are added regularly

## What to prioritize first

Start with these high-value tools immediately after approval:

- **JetBrains**: IntelliJ IDEA, PyCharm, CLion, WebStorm (free Pro licenses)
- **DigitalOcean**: ₹7,000+ in cloud credits
- **AWS**: ₹27,000+ in credits if you link your account
- **Namecheap**: free .me domain for a year
- **GitHub Copilot**: included with GitHub Pro

## After the pack

Once you've claimed the Student Pack, other free resources:

- **Google Cloud**: their own student credit program (separate from the pack)
- **Microsoft Azure**: student credit pathway
- **Figma**: free Education plan for design
- **Notion**: student discount on Plus plan

The Developer Pack is just the start — many vendors have their own student programs if you ask.

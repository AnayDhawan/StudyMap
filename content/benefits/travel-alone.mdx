# Travelling alone within India as a student

> Placeholder guide. Verify rules with official sources (IRCTC, airlines, hostels) before relying on this.

Travelling solo for an exam, an olympiad, or a campus visit is very doable. A little planning makes it smooth.

## Before you go

- Carry a government photo ID. For most domestic travel a school ID plus an Aadhaar card works; check the specific requirement.
- Keep digital and printed copies of your tickets and ID.
- Share your live location and itinerary with a parent or guardian.

## Trains

- Book through IRCTC. Choose a confirmed berth rather than a waitlist for long journeys.
- For overnight trains, prefer an upper berth and keep valuables close.

## Flights

- Airlines have their own rules for minors. Many allow students above a certain age to fly unaccompanied with ID; confirm with the airline before booking.
- Reach the airport early for your first few solo flights.

## Staying safe

- Prefer well-reviewed hostels or guesthouses near your venue.
- Keep some cash plus a backup payment method.
- Save local emergency numbers and your venue address offline.
